Rizal's Role in the Propaganda Movement

Rizal was a key figure in the Propaganda Movement, which sought reforms in the Philippines under Spanish rule. He contributed to the movement by publishing articles, essays, and books that criticized the Spanish government and clergy, advocating for the rights and freedoms of Filipinos.

He collaborated with other reformists like Graciano Lopez Jaena and Marcelo del Pilar in advocating for political and social change. Through his writings, Rizal influenced the formation of revolutionary ideas that led to the Philippine Revolution of 1896.
